NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 — Function Mapping

NIST CSF 2.0 provides a comprehensive framework organised around six core functions: Govern (GV), Identify (ID), Protect (PR), Detect (DE), Respond (RS), and Recover (RC). Each function contains categories and subcategories that define desired cybersecurity outcomes. Below is the mapping of Microsoft Defender XDR capabilities to NIST CSF subcategories.

GOVERN GV — Organisational Context, Strategy & Governance

3 subcategories mapped ▼
GV.OC-03 Legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements are understood and managed Partial XDR Secure Score
GV.RR-02 Cybersecurity roles and responsibilities are established and coordinated Partial XDR Multi-Tenant Management
GV.RR-03 Adequate resources are allocated for cybersecurity Partial XDR Secure Score

IDENTIFY ID — Asset Management, Risk Assessment

5 subcategories mapped ▼
ID.AM-01 Inventories of hardware managed by the organisation are maintained Compliant MDE Device Inventory
ID.AM-02 Inventories of software, services, and systems managed by the organisation are maintained Compliant MDVM Software Inventory, MDCA Shadow IT, MDVM Browser Ext.
ID.RA-01 Vulnerabilities in assets are identified, validated, and recorded Compliant MDVM Vulnerability Assessment
ID.RA-02 Cyber threat intelligence is received from information sharing forums and sources Compliant XDR Threat Analytics Reports
ID.GV-03 Cybersecurity is included in human resources practices Partial XDR Secure Score

PROTECT PR — Access Control, Data Security, Platform Security

10 subcategories mapped ▼
PR.AC-04 Access permissions and authorisations are managed Compliant MDCA Session Controls, XDR RBAC
PR.AC-06 Identities are proofed, bound to credentials, and managed Compliant MDI AD Security, MDCA OAuth Monitoring
PR.AC-07 Users, devices, and other assets are authenticated commensurate with risk Compliant MDI Credential Detection
PR.AT-01 Personnel are provided awareness and training so they can perform security-related duties Partial MDO Anti-Phishing Policies
PR.DS-01 Data-at-rest is protected Compliant MDE Controlled Folder Access
PR.DS-02 Data-in-transit is protected Partial MDE Network Protection, MDO DMARC/DKIM/SPF
PR.IP-01 Baseline configurations of systems are created and maintained Partial MDI DC Sensor Deployment, MDVM Security Baselines
PR.PT-03 The principle of least functionality is incorporated Compliant MDE ASR Rules, MDE Web Content Filtering, MDO Safe Links

DETECT DE — Continuous Monitoring, Adverse Event Analysis

4 subcategories mapped ▼
DE.CM-01 Networks and network services are monitored to find potentially adverse events Compliant MDE EDR, MDO Safe Attachments
DE.CM-03 Personnel activity and technology usage are monitored Compliant MDI Lateral Movement Detection
DE.AE-02 Potentially adverse events are analysed to better understand associated activities Compliant MDI Identity Threat Detection, XDR Advanced Hunting
DE.AE-04 The estimated impact and scope of adverse events is understood Compliant XDR Unified Incident Queue

RESPOND RS — Incident Management, Analysis, Mitigation

3 subcategories mapped ▼

RS.AN-03 Analysis is performed to establish what has taken place during an incident Compliant MDE Automated Investigation & Remediation
RS.MI-01 Incidents are contained Compliant MDO Zero-hour Auto Purge (ZAP)

0 directly mapped ▼
